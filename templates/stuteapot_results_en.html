What is the meaning-oriented learning style?

According to Vermunt, when learning is oriented towards the creation of meaning, knowledge is processed by relating the details to the subject matter as a whole and structuring it. The knowledge content is processed critically. It is often paired with an awareness and a capacity of evaluating how you learn and what you learn.

Learning is associated to the mental model of constructing knowledge. Moreover, a meaning-oriented learning style is motivated by personal interest in the subject matters.


What is the reproduction-oriented learning style?

The reproduction-oriented learning style favours an analytical, organised approach to studying. Knowledge is processed by memorising and rehearsing, and by analysing theories or problems step by step.

Because learning is thought as an intake of knowledge, studying means making sure the knowledge has been received. This often leads to a need of external regulation, for instance verifying if a concept is understood or if a set of items is memorized entirely by completing a mock-exam or practicing on an exercise sheet.

Learning is motivated by either obtaining a certificate, a grade or a degree (depending on the scale) or testing one-self, hence thinking of academic pursuit as a challenge.


What is the application-oriented learning style?

The application-oriented learning style is maybe the most forward one as it is characterised by a need for concretising knowledge and sense the use one can make of it. Studying is learning how to use knowledge, how to apply it to concrete situations.

It is often paired with a motivation that comes from something concrete still – obtaining a certificate – or that comes from a rather precise idea of why learning is needed which can be a vocation for a future job.


What is the undirected learning style?

When learning is undirected, the lack of a clear motivation makes it difficult to be aware one’s own learning processes, goals and overall relation to knowledge. There is often a lack of regulation, that’s to say a lack of perspective and of concrete actions to evaluate one’s knowledge and learning methods or strategies

It is often associated with an ambivalent learning orientation, and somehow a difficulty to understand the point of studies. Learning may be thought simply as a stimulating activity. It may also be viewed a as cooperation, but where others take the lead or even that leads to nowhere precisely.
